Amend thy style who can: who can amend thy style?
    For sweet conceit.
    Alas the while,
That ever any such, as thou shouldst die,
    By Fortune's guile,
    Amids thy meat.
Pardon (Oh pardon) me that cannot show,
    My zealous love.
    Yet shalt thou prove,
That I will ever write in thy behoove:
    Gainst any dare,
    With thee compare.
Art is not Hodge-poke nor his fellow deer,
    That I do fear:
