"Let all the creatures of this earth
    Or hail thy smile, or dread thy frown!"
Nature exclaimed, when man had birth,
    And on his cradle placed her crown.

"This globe be subject to thy tread,
    You stars to thy command;
Be thine the all-contriving head,
    And all-performing hand!" -- 

So Nature spoke, with voice benign,
    When, from her blackest cave,
Bigotry yelled, "A share be mine,
    From cradle to the grave!"

The Sun of Reason then began
    To set, eclipsed in blood;
